Accountability of the Data Set

WHO's Global Health Observatory (GHO) database contains data on various themes including- progress towards the Millenium Development Goals (the 8 MDGs), Health Systems (workforce data, financing data, etc.), Non-Communicable Diseases (Mortality/ Morbity, Health Risk Factors, Health System Response), Substance Use and Mental Health, Infectious Diseases, and Injuries and Violence. I concentrate on a dataset describing the Density of Health Workforce recorded across countries over the last three decades across different countries.

Source of the Data

Estimation

WHO compiles data on health workforce from four major sources:

  1. population censuses
  2. labour force and employment surveys
  3. health facility assessments
  4. routine administrative information systems (including reports on public expenditure, staffing and payroll as well as professional training, registration and licensure)
Most of the data from administrative sources are derived from published national health sector reviews and/or official country reports to WHO offices In general, the denominator data for workforce density (i.e. national population estimates) are obtained from the United Nations Population Division's World Population Prospects database. Goal of Dataset

To prepare the health workforce across different countries throughout the world to work towards the attainment of their country's health objectives represents one of the most important challenges for its health system. [1]

Access

Much of the WHO Data is publicly accessible. The GHO data repository contains an extensive list of indicators, which can be selected by theme or through a multi-dimension query functionality. It is the World Health Organization's main health statistics repository. [1]
