Relation Between DTP3 Immunization and Availability of nursing and Midwifery personnel across countries

Immunization is listed by the World Health Organization (WHO) as one of the 8 MGDs (Millenium Development Goals).

The Data

Map 1 shows the percentage of children immunized for one of the most fundamental vaccines viz. Diphtheria Tetanus Toxoid and Pertussis or DTP, which is typically administered to infants within their first year of age. The dataset comprised of immunized percentages between 1980 and 2014. The averge value across countries has increased over time. Also, the variance of this data has decreased. While most commonwealth and developing countries recorded poor levels of DTP vaccination in 1980 starting from 1%(Bangladesh), these nations have improved significantly and come at par with the more affluent nations by 2014. Bangladesh now stands at a healthy 95%. However, there still are a few nations in Central Africa and the Gulf region, that continue to have low rates of DTP immunity. Equatorial Guinea currently records the lowest at 24%.

Map 2 shows the availability of nursing and midwifery by country, per thousand citizens.
